# GenomeAI Vision

## Mission

To democratize genomic intelligence by building the world's most comprehensive open-source platform for genomics, bioinformatics, biomedical research, and evidence-based AI — making genome-scale analysis as accessible as a database query.

## Why GenomeAI?

Genomic data is doubling every seven months. The gap between data generation and actionable insight is widening. Proprietary platforms lock research behind licensing fees. Fragmented tooling forces researchers to stitch together dozens of incompatible libraries. Reproducibility remains an afterthought.

GenomeAI exists to close these gaps.

## Core Principles

### 1. Open by Default

Every component — from the ingestion engine to the ML serving layer — is open source. We build in public, welcome contributions, and refuse to gatekeep genomic research behind proprietary interfaces.

### 2. Evidence-Based AI

We do not chase black-box models. Every prediction, classification, and recommendation must be traceable to underlying evidence — whether from literature, clinical databases, or first-principles biology. Explainability is a first-class requirement, not an afterthought.

### 3. Modular and Composable

No monoliths. GenomeAI is a platform of interoperable components. Researchers swap out aligners, embed custom ML models, and build plugins without forking the core. Every module defines clear contracts and can be used independently.

### 4. Reproducible by Design

Analyses will be expressed as directed acyclic graphs of versioned steps. Inputs, parameters, code, and environment should be captured automatically, enabling reconstruction of past results.

### 5. Privacy and Security

Genomic data is among the most sensitive personal data that exists. GenomeAI aims to support on-premise, air-gapped, and compliant deployment from the start. Differential privacy, attribute-based access control, and audit logging are planned as core platform capabilities.

### 6. Scale from Laptop to Cluster

The same pipeline that runs on a researcher's laptop scales to thousands of cores on HPC or Kubernetes. The platform adapts to available resources without requiring users to rewrite workflows.

## The Long View

| Horizon | Goal |
|---------|------|
| **v0.1–v0.5** | Foundational pipelines: WGS, WES, RNA-seq, and single-cell. Core knowledge graph online. Plugin SDK v1. |
| **v1.0–v1.5** | Clinical decision support modules. Real-time population-scale analytics. Federated learning across institutions. |
| **v2.0–v2.5** | Whole-genome simulation and perturbation. Integration with electronic health records. Automated hypothesis generation via LLM agents grounded in the knowledge graph. |
| **v3.0+** | A global, open, real-time genomic intelligence network connecting research, clinical, and public health institutions — enabling discoveries that no single lab could make alone. |
